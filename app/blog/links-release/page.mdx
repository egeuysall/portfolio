# Links v1.1 is Here!

I'm super excited to share that **Links v1.1** is now live! This new update brings a bunch of cool features and improvements to my open-source alternative to Linktree. If you're looking for an easy, customizable way to bring all your important links into one neat page, Links is definitely worth checking out.

**[Get Links here!](https://www.links.egeuysal.com/)**

## What Makes Links Special?

Links is all about giving you control over your online presence. Unlike other tools out there, you’re not locked into some template or premium plan—everything is free and customizable. I built Links to be simple, clean, and fully in your control. Whether you're a creator, freelancer, or small business owner, Links is designed to help you share your content the way you want.

The best part? It’s open-source, so you can even modify it if you want to make it your own.

## What’s New in Version 1.1?

In this update, I’m excited to introduce **personalized button styling**. Now, you can adjust the shape of your buttons to fit your style. Whether you want them rounded, slightly curved, or with sharp corners, you have the freedom to design it exactly how you like.

On top of that, I’ve made the platform even better with a more polished design and a cleaner layout. I’ve added a landing page that shows off all the things Links can do, making it easier to see what the platform offers. Plus, if you're someone who loves customizing everything, you can now export your generated code for advanced tweaks.

There’s also a bunch of new resources, like detailed guides and tips, to help you get the most out of Links. I’ve redesigned the user interface to be more intuitive and easier to navigate, so you'll spend less time fiddling with settings and more time sharing your links.

## Creating Your Own Links Page

Creating a Links page is simple. Just head over to [links.egeuysal.com](http://links.egeuysal.com), where you’ll find an easy-to-use editor to design your page. Once you’ve customized it to your liking, you can grab the source code and deploy it anywhere.

I made sure there are two quick ways to publish your page. You can use **Vercel** for super fast deployment with just one click—great for beginners. If you’re familiar with GitHub, **GitHub Pages** is a solid free option, and I’ve written up a simple guide on how to get that set up too.
